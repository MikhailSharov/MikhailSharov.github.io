Rating System: I will be rating the books on a scale of 0-5 with 0.5 increments. This rating system will be based soley on my opinion of the book in question and does not necesserily mean I recommend others to read the book. There are instances where I may enjoy a book and yet not recommend it. A 3 indicates an average book which held my interest until the end. A 0 means I strongly disliked the book, and a 5 means I strongly liked the book.

January 17 - 24: The Pale Blue Dot by Carl Sagan

Non-fiction, Like to Book

Admittedly, this is not the first time I have read this book. However I greatly enjoy it. My own passion for space exploration leads me to naturally enjoy this book. The eloquent way in which Sagan explains the frailty and minisclue impact of humanity is amazing. The awe of the scale of everything is what drives me to persue my career and this book sums it up excellently. On top of this, the visions of the future, both optimistic and scary which Sagan provides are subjects which matter and should be read about. This book continually inspires me and rightly earns its score.

Score: 4.5 / 5
